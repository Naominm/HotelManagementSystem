Chief chef Dash Delicious

A Buxton, Maine native, Danielle Henry
I am small-town girl with a big city attitude.
My two lifelong passions are baking and cats.
Classically trained in the former from Johnson and Whales University,
I have taken my talents throughout Maine,
from the exciting fast paced restaurants of Portland
to the sleepy shores of \Vinalhaven Island off the coast of Rockland,
and everywhere in between.
My work as a pastry chef has been featured in both Downeast magazine
and the Portland Press Herald.
my aim is to share my experiences through great story telling,
passion for learning, teaching, having fun and my love of travel.
I hope to one day be able to travel the globe,
trying every dessert and petting every cat!

Jane Mwangi

Chef


Possessing a genuine passion for cooking from a young age,
I was only 15 when I began working in the food industry at the Muffin Shop in Marblehead, Massachusetts.
I graduated in 2007 from Johnson & Wales University
and returned to Boston to begin my career at the Top of the Hub restaurant.
From there, I went to work with Barbara Lynch Gruppo as a line cook at B&G Oysters,
where I quickly rose through the ranks to the position of sous chef.

I continued to further on my fine-dining skills at restaurants like Stir and No. 9 Park,
eventually showcasing my culinary prowess by competing on Top Chef.
After the show, I opened the pop-up Stacked Donuts in Boston.
In 2015, I joined Fairsted Kitchen as the executive chef,
crafting a menu that reflects her personal cooking style with French
and Italian dishes that have a Middle Eastern spin. After a year at Fairsted,
I took a position at the Venetian restaurant SRV in Boston’s South End,
where I worked to master the art of making pasta. During that time I also worked part time as a private chef and discovered that she loved the creative freedom of working for Myself and cooking for small groups.
